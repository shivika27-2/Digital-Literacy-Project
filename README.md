## Project Overview

This project demonstrates practical digital literacy skills across five structured tasks. Each task targets a distinct area of digital competence — from visual design and professional networking, to coding practice, email etiquette, and cybercrime awareness. The goal is to build a well-rounded digital identity and develop responsible, informed online habits from the very first year of study.

---

## Module Summaries

### Task 1 – Digital Literacy Infographic
Designed a structured vertical infographic using **Canva** titled *"Digital Literacy: Your Guide to Navigating the Online World."* The infographic covers the definition of digital literacy, useful student tools (Google Docs, Trello, Quizlet), and safe internet practices such as using strong passwords and protecting personal information.


---

### Task 2 – Digital Portfolio Setup
Created and set up profiles on three key platforms to establish a professional digital presence:
- **GitHub** – For version control, academic assignments, and personal coding projects.
- **LinkedIn** – For professional networking, internship applications, and industry connections.
- **Kaggle** – For data science learning, micro-courses, and ML competition participation.



---

### Task 3 – Online Tools in Practice
Explored two platforms serving distinct academic and professional purposes:
- **HackerRank** – Completed the *Arithmetic Operators* Python challenge (10 pts), contributing to Python badge progress.
- **Google Forms** – Built a five-question *Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz* linked to Google Sheets for automatic response collection.

### Task 4 – Email Etiquette & Digital Communication
Studied professional email writing and responsible social media use. Produced two polished emails and a social media checklist:

- 📧 **Email 1** – Request for Assignment Deadline Extension (Fundamentals of AIML)
- 📧 **Email 2** – Summer Internship Application to Microsoft
- ✅ **Social Media Checklist** – 6 Do's and 6 Don'ts for responsible use as a college student

**Key Case Study:** The *Cisco Fat* incident — a real-world example of how a single public post can permanently damage professional reputation.



---

### Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness
Researched UPI fraud as a prevalent form of cybercrime targeting students in India. Key findings:
- Most UPI fraud requires no technical skills — it exploits confusion between sending and receiving money.
- India's **National Cyber Crime Helpline 1930** exists for fast financial fraud reporting but remains largely unknown among students.

**Personal Commitment:** To carefully read every UPI transaction screen before entering a PIN, and never act under urgency or pressure during digital payments.

🔗 [National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in)
