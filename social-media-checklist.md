# Social Media Checklist – Responsible Use for College Students

## ✅ Do's

| # | Do |
|---|-----|
| 1 | **Think before you post** – Ask yourself if the content is something you'd be comfortable with a professor, employer, or family member seeing. |
| 2 | **Keep your profiles professional** – Use a clear profile photo and maintain a bio that reflects your academic and career interests. |
| 3 | **Give credit where it's due** – Always tag or mention the original creator when sharing or reposting someone else's work or ideas. |
| 4 | **Use privacy settings wisely** – Regularly review who can see your posts, stories, and personal information on each platform. |
| 5 | **Engage respectfully** – Participate in discussions with courtesy and an open mind, even when you disagree with someone's opinion. |
| 6 | **Fact-check before sharing** – Verify information from a reliable source before reposting news, statistics, or claims. |

---

## ❌ Don'ts

| # | Don't |
|---|-------|
| 1 | **Don't share personal or sensitive information** – Avoid posting your phone number, home address, ID numbers, or daily schedule publicly. |
| 2 | **Don't engage in cyberbullying or hate speech** – Never post content that targets, demeans, or harasses individuals or groups. |
| 3 | **Don't plagiarise or misrepresent content** – Do not pass off others' photos, writing, or ideas as your own without proper attribution. |
| 4 | **Don't overshare in the moment** – Avoid posting real-time locations, especially when travelling alone or in unfamiliar places. |
| 5 | **Don't let social media disrupt your academics** – Avoid excessive use during study hours, classes, or assignment deadlines. |
| 6 | **Don't respond impulsively to negativity** – Avoid engaging in online arguments or reacting emotionally to trolls or provocative comments. |
